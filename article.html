NOVEMBER 18, 2025 · MILESTONE

SVX validates a precursor-free process that halves CapEx and secures U.S. supply chains.

San Francisco Bay Area — Sylvatex (SVX) has validated a precursor-free route to cathode active material that, in internal benchmarking, roughly halves the capital expenditure required to build new production capacity while relying on domestic feedstocks.

Conventional cathode production depends on a precursor stage that is among the most capital- and carbon-intensive parts of the battery supply chain. By removing that stage and running a waterless process on off-the-shelf equipment, SVX reduces both the upfront capital and the operating footprint of a plant.

"Solving the cathode is the highest-leverage move available to anyone trying to scale batteries affordably and cleanly in the West."

Why it matters

The cathode is the single largest driver of both cost and carbon in a lithium-ion battery. A process that lowers the cost of capacity and shortens the path to domestic production directly supports the resilience of the U.S. battery supply chain across mobility, grid storage, and defense.

  • Lower capital needed to build and scale new plants
  • Domestic feedstocks supporting regionalized supply chains
  • A waterless route that removes wastewater and lowers emissions
  • Chemistry-agnostic output across NMC, LFP and NCA

What's next

SVX is operating at pilot scale and collaborating with top-tier EV manufacturers and supply-chain partners on qualifying materials and further scaling the process toward commercial volumes.
